Melbourne, Australia

This graph displays a comparison between three datasets, with the purpose being to easily and visually be able to pick out deviation from weather predictions to real weather.

This data allows you to visualise to what degree weather reports become more accurate over time, and how regular outliers are.

The seven-day prediction is the reported weather in seven days, and will always be out in front of the graph.

NOTE:
The data between May 1st and may 10th is fabricated for the sake of testing.
Time zones also cause issues, as the data is taken in Unix time but displayed in local time zones. I assume that dependant on your timezone this would have a less or more significant effect. Also because the dates are stored as the ISO UTC standard but the predictions data is relevant to noon AEST, some quirks are caused. I assume anyway, for it is really difficult to test.
